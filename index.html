Splinting

What is a splint?

A splint is a device used to hold extremities stable and rigid to prevent pain and further injuries.

When is a splint used?

Fractured long bone Dislocation Limited range of motion Pain in extremities or joints

Why would you use a splint?

Decrease a patient’s pain. Prevent further injuries

How do you apply a splint?

For long bones: Immobilized joints above and below For joints: Immobilized long bones above and below Position the injured area in a comfortable position Don’t attempt to reposition the patient Always check CMS before and after splinting

CMS Check (Circulation, Motor, Sensation)

Circulation: Check pulse, blood pressure, capillary refill test Motor: Ask the patient to move parts of their body (fingers, toes…) Sensation: Lightly touch the area for feedback

Sam Splints

Form length and shape using the patient’s opposite limb or your own Have the victim assist in securing the splint if able Secure with bandages, distal to proximal

Bandages

Support, compress, and secure the splint Leave a 6-inch tail to apply tension Switch hands at the midline of the body Wrap so each turn overlaps the previous by half Wrap perpendicular to the body part
